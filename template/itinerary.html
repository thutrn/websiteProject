8:00 AM - Breakfast at The Art We Are Cafe

Start your day with a cozy breakfast at The Art We Are Cafe, a local favorite with great coffee, artisanal breakfast items, and an artistic atmosphere. Try their signature granola bowls, fresh baked goods, or a hearty breakfast burrito. It's a great spot to fuel up before heading out into the cold.

9:00 AM - Snowshoeing or Cross-Country Skiing at Stake Lake

After breakfast, head to Stake Lake, a beautiful area just 20 minutes outside of Kamloops, known for its cross-country skiing and snowshoeing trails. You can rent gear at the Stake Lake Nordic Centre if you don’t have your own equipment. Snowshoeing: Explore the peaceful trails surrounded by snow-covered trees and winter wildlife. Cross-Country Skiing: Kamloops is known for its well-groomed ski trails, and Stake Lake offers over 30 km of classic and skate skiing tracks. It's a great place to enjoy the crisp winter air and get some exercise.

12:00 PM - Hot Lunch at The Noble Pig Brewhouse

After a morning of outdoor activities, head back to Kamloops for lunch. The Noble Pig Brewhouse is a great spot to enjoy a warm, hearty meal. They offer a variety of craft beers brewed on-site and a seasonal menu with comfort food like wood-fired pizza, gourmet burgers, and hearty soups—perfect after a morning in the snow.

1:30 PM - Visit the Kamloops Heritage Railway & Railway Museum

Next, head over to the Kamloops Heritage Railway for a bit of local history. Depending on the time of year, you can either take a winter train ride or visit the museum to learn about the region's rail history. The Kamloops Railway Museum has interesting exhibits and artifacts, showcasing how the railroad played a crucial role in the development of the region.

3:00 PM - Relax at Sun Peaks Resort

If you're in the mood for more outdoor activities or want to enjoy some après-ski time, head up to Sun Peaks Resort, about 45 minutes from Kamloops. Even if you're not skiing or snowboarding, you can take a scenic ride on the village gondola for breathtaking winter views, or simply enjoy the resort's charming alpine village. If you'd rather stay in Kamloops, you can opt to take a scenic drive around Kamloops Lake or visit Riverside Park for a peaceful winter walk along the river.

5:00 PM - Coffee or Hot Chocolate at Cafe Motivo

On your way back into town, stop by Café Motivo, a cozy local spot known for its specialty coffee and hot chocolate drinks. It’s the perfect place to warm up, relax, and watch the winter world outside.

6:30 PM - Dinner at Teriyaki Town

For dinner, try Teriyaki Town, a favorite for local Japanese food. Their dishes are flavorful and hearty—perfect for the winter season. If you’re in the mood for something more casual, you could also grab a delicious sushi roll or teriyaki rice bowl.

8:00 PM - Evening Walk and Stargazing at Kenna Cartwright Park

Finish your day with a peaceful winter walk in Kenna Cartwright Park, which is just a short drive from downtown Kamloops. The park offers panoramic views of the city and, in the winter months, the crisp, clear skies make for fantastic stargazing. If the skies are clear, this is the perfect place to enjoy the beauty of the night sky and the winter landscape.

9:30 PM - Nightcap at The Commodore Grand Cafe & Lounge

To wrap up your evening, head to The Commodore Grand Café & Lounge for a nightcap. This historical spot has a relaxed, upscale atmosphere and is perfect for a drink before bed. Enjoy a local craft cocktail or a glass of wine from the nearby Okanagan region.
