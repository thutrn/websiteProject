What is the weather like in Kamloops?

Start your day with a cozy breakfast at The Art We Are Cafe, a local favorite with great coffee, artisanal breakfast items, and an artistic atmosphere. Try their signature granola bowls, fresh baked goods, or a hearty breakfast burrito. It's a great spot to fuel up before heading out into the cold.

Can I rent outdoor equipment in Kamloops?

After breakfast, head to Stake Lake, a beautiful area just 20 minutes outside of Kamloops, known for its cross-country skiing and snowshoeing trails. You can rent gear at the Stake Lake Nordic Centre if you don't have your own equipment. Snowshoeing: Explore the peaceful trails surrounded by snow-covered trees and winter wildlife. Cross-Country Skiing: Kamloops is known for its well-groomed ski trails, and Stake Lake offers over 30 km of classic and skate skiing tracks. It's a great place to enjoy the crisp winter air and get some exercise.

How do I get to Kamloops?

By car: Kamloops is located on the Trans-Canada Highway (Highway 1) and is about a 3.5-hour drive from Vancouver. Check out for flights
By air: Kamloops has an international airport (Kamloops Airport — YKA) with regular flights from Vancouver and other major cities.
By bus: Greyhound and other regional bus services operate routes to Kamloops.

What Kamloops is known for?

Kamloops is known for its beautiful natural landscapes, outdoor activities, and vibrant culture. It is a popular destination for skiing, hiking, moutain biking, and enjoying scenic lakes and rivers. Kamloops is also famous for its rich history and is often referred to as the Tournament Capital of Canada due to its many sports tournaments.

What are the top attractions in Kamloops?

Sun Peaks Resort: A world-class ski resort offering year-round activities.
Riverside Park: A beautiful park with walking trails and picnic areas along the Thompson River.
Kamloops Heritage Railway: A chance to ride a vintage train through the stunning landscape.
The BC Wildlife Park: A family-friendly attraction showcasing native wildlife.
Kamloops Art Gallery: A vibrant space for contemporary art exhibits.

Variety of Activities in Kamloops?

Skiing and Snowboarding at Sun Peaks Resort.
Hiking and Mountain Biking on numerous trails around the city and surrounding areas.
Golfing on world-class golf courses.
Water Sports on Kamloops Lake and the Thompson River, including kayaking, fishing, and boating.
Wildlife Viewing in the nearby wilderness and at the BC Wildlife Park.

Ideal time for Activities in Kamloops?

Winter (December to March): Ideal for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports at Sun Peaks Resort.
Spring (April to June): Great for hiking, biking, and enjoying outdoor festivals.
Summer (July to September): Perfect for exploring lakes, beaches, and enjoying warm weather activities.
Fall (October to November): A scenic time to visit with beautiful autumn colors and mild weather for outdoor activities.

What are some local food and drink specialities in Kamloops?

Local Wine: Kamloops is home to several wineries offering award-winning wines.
Craft Beer: The city has a growing craft beer scene, with several local breweries.
Farm-to-Table Dining: Enjoy locally sourced meats, vegetables, and other fresh ingredients at various restaurants in the area.
Thompson River Salmon: Try locally caught fish and other regional specialties.
